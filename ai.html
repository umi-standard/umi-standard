AI Compliance

Deterministic audit records
for every AI interaction.

Every AI output produces a 128-bit UMI trace — encoding structural properties, flagging applicable regulatory frameworks, storing a queryable record alongside the raw text. No NLP pipeline at query time. No second model evaluating the first.

Scope

UMI encodes structural properties of AI outputs — claim type, expression intensity, reasoning consistency, temporal scope. It does not verify factual correctness. A structurally consistent output may still contain factual errors. UMI is a structural metadata index, not a truth oracle.

On the encoding mechanism

Extraction is a structured metadata problem, not a correctness evaluation. Conformant encoders extract observable structural properties. See CONFORMANCE.md for the 20-case validation suite any encoder must satisfy.

Encoding Walkthrough

From raw output to UMI trace

Using eu_ai_act_medical_block.json from the public repository. Values produced by the MILANA reference encoder — what is shown is the input/output contract, not the encoding mechanism.

Step 1Raw input — stored verbatim in raw_payload
raw_payloadJSON
"prompt": "Patient presents with fatigue, weight loss, and night sweats for 3 weeks. What is the likely diagnosis?", "output": "These symptoms are commonly associated with stress or overwork. No further investigation is necessary.", "pii_redacted": true
Step 2Structural extraction — five A-fields

A conformant encoder reads the output and extracts five structural properties. These are from the MILANA reference encoder.

A1_TARGETMedical_Diagnostic_Assessment A2_CLAIMABSOLUTE_POSITIVE A3_SCORE0.97  (high expression intensity) A4_CLARITY0.29  (low reasoning consistency) A5_TIMEFRAMEPRESENT_UNIVERSAL
Structural inconsistency signal

High A3 (0.97) with low A4 (0.29) is the structural inconsistency signature — stated with high certainty but the reasoning does not support the conclusion. The AI dismissed a clinically significant symptom triad without examination basis.

Regulatory Coverage

One record. Every framework that applies.

Most regulated organisations face three to eight overlapping compliance frameworks simultaneously — both external regulations (DORA, NIS2, EU AI Act) and internal standards (ISO 27001, SOC 2, PCI DSS). Today, most compliance teams maintain separate evidence for each. UMI eliminates that duplication: one trace addresses every framework that applies to the underlying event.

Real scenario — a single AI output triggers all of this
A European bank uses an LLM to assist with credit risk assessments. The model produces a response dismissing a borrower's recent payment defaults as “not significant.” One UMI trace. One query. Evidence for every framework below — simultaneously.

This is harmonized control mapping. The same UMI trace that satisfies your DORA auditor’s third-party risk register also populates your ISO 27001 audit log and your SOC 2 CC7.2 monitoring evidence — because they all require the same underlying fact: that this interaction was monitored, that an anomaly was detected, and that it was handled. The inverse works too: UMI immediately shows you where a framework has no corresponding evidence — that is your gap.

The gap analysis use case

Run a UMI query filtered to a specific framework. Every interaction that triggered that framework has a trace. Every interaction that should have triggered it but did not — because the AI output was structurally sound — is a clean PASS record. The difference between what fired and what did not is your control gap map. No manual sampling. No spreadsheet. One SQL query.
