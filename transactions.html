Financial Transactions

Transaction pattern deviation
detection via UMI.

UMI applies to financial transaction streams with zero schema changes. A4_CLARITY measures structural deviation from an account's own baseline transaction graph — the same 128-bit envelope, the same SQL query pattern, the same compliance record format used for AI outputs and log analysis.

Register interest ↓ Try the Explorer About UMI
Scope

UMI encodes structural deviation signals from transaction data. It does not perform fraud determination, replace AML compliance programmes, or constitute regulatory approval. Interpretation and action remain with qualified compliance teams and qualified humans.

Why it matters

Three problems UMI actually solves

Payment Compliance Officer
A failed SCA event is simultaneously a PSD2 audit record, a potential DORA incident, and a GDPR Art.22 automated decision log.
Today those are three separate evidence trails maintained by three teams. One UMI trace addresses all three simultaneously. When the regulator calls, you run one query — not three manual exports from three systems.
Fraud & AML Analyst
You flagged the transaction. Can you prove you flagged it when it happened — not after the investigation started?
A UMI trace is a tamper-evident, timestamped, checksummed record. It documents that structural deviation was detected at the moment it occurred. That is the difference between a defensible audit trail and a retrospective reconstruction.
Fintech Architect
16 bytes per transaction.
Standard SQL.
No second model. No rule engine refresh cycle.
One indexed column added to your transaction table. Query by A4_CLARITY below threshold. Every anomalous transaction surfaced in milliseconds. Your existing SIEM, your existing database, your existing compliance workflow — UMI plugs in as a structured signal layer.
Find every structural anomaly in a payment batch — PSD2 and DORA simultaneouslySQL
-- One query. Two regulatory obligations. No manual review. SELECT trace_id, transaction_id, timestamp_utc, (semantic_summary->>'coherence_score')::float AS a4_clarity, regulatory_flags FROM umi_traces WHERE substrate = 'transaction' AND (semantic_summary->>'coherence_score')::float < 0.50 AND timestamp_utc > NOW() - INTERVAL '24 hours' ORDER BY a4_clarity ASC; -- Returns: DORA threshold candidates AND PSD2 anomaly log. -- Same trace. Same query. Both obligations covered.

Use Cases

Six transaction analysis domains

Payment Anomaly

Transaction Pattern Deviation

A4_CLARITY measures structural deviation from the account's own baseline — amount, frequency, counterparty, geography, time of day. One tunable threshold, deterministic output.

Bank Statements

Statement Coherence

Structural properties from statement sequences — income step changes, circular payment patterns. Every analysis is a GDPR Art. 22 automated decision record by design.

AML / CFT

Structuring Signal

Structural deviation signal for transaction structuring patterns. A supplementary signal alongside existing AML programmes — not a replacement. Adds a queryable audit layer to existing detection.

DORA Art. 17

ICT Payment Incident

Classifies payment processing failures as potential DORA major ICT incidents — duplicate transactions, settlement failures, reconciliation gaps. The UMI trace is the classification evidence.

PSD2

Authentication Anomaly

Authentication patterns deviating from account baseline. The UMI trace is the PSD2 Art. 98 strong customer authentication audit record — timestamped and checksummed at the moment of detection.

MiCA

Crypto-Asset Transfer

Structural deviation signals for stablecoin and crypto-asset flows. MiCA Art. 19 communication integrity record alongside market integrity monitoring — same schema, no extra infrastructure.
