Commercial implementations
built on the UMI standard.

MILANA  ·  AiQUS  ·  VELANA  ·  UMI Initiative

The VELANA Project is building the commercial intelligence layer that supports deterministic audit traces across AI outputs, network infrastructure, and financial transaction streams. The open UMI standard is the foundation. MILANA, AiQUS, and VELANA are what you build on top of it.

Important separation

The UMI standard is open and Apache 2.0 licensed. MILANA, AiQUS, and VELANA are proprietary commercial implementations built on that standard. Any organisation may build a conformant UMI encoder independently. The standard does not depend on any VELANA Project component. See GOVERNANCE.md.

Limitations

UMI encodes structural properties of events. It does not determine factual correctness, establish regulatory compliance, or replace domain-specific validation systems. Compliance determination remains with qualified humans. The UMI trace is evidence input to that determination — not the determination itself.

The structural insight

Why substrate agnosticism is not a product decision.

Cross-domain structural hypothesis — working implementation, formal justification forthcoming
UMI is built on the hypothesis that a shared set of structural properties — modality, certainty, temporal scope, consistency signal — can be extracted across fundamentally different classes of text input: AI outputs, system logs, and transaction descriptions. The current implementation applies a single schema across all three domains and demonstrates consistent behaviour in practice. Whether this abstraction fully generalises is an open question — and part of the ongoing research.
A formal treatment of the underlying extraction model is currently in preparation for peer-reviewed publication. Early results are consistent with the cross-domain behaviour demonstrated in the UMI Explorer. Until that work is published, the claim rests on observable behaviour — which you can verify across all three substrates in the Explorer today, using identical schema output.

The practical consequence of the hypothesis is visible on the other pages of this site: a consistent query pattern can be applied to support compliance workflows across frameworks such as NIS2, DORA, ISO 27001, and SOC 2 — across AI outputs, network logs, and transaction data — because the underlying trace structure is currently identical for all three substrates. If the formal justification holds under peer review, the implications extend beyond compliance infrastructure. That work is ongoing. This page will be updated when it is published.

Design principles

What we believe about infrastructure.

01

Determinism over probability

Compliance records must produce the same output for the same input, every time. Probabilistic scoring is appropriate for recommendations. It is not appropriate for regulatory evidence. A record that might be different tomorrow is not a record.

02

Edge over cloud

Sensitive data should ultimately be processed where it is generated — at the server, the firewall, or the API gateway. Today the encoding is available as an API. Edge deployment — no data leaving your environment — is the roadmap destination.

03

Standards over lock-in

The UMI format is Apache 2.0. Any organisation may build a conformant encoder. The standard must outlast any single vendor, including the VELANA Project itself. This is not a marketing position. It is in the GOVERNANCE.md.

04

Substrate agnosticism

A compliance standard that only covers AI outputs is incomplete. The networks, databases, and payment systems that AI operates on are designed to apply the same standard, the same schema, and the same query pattern. One schema. Every substrate.

Provenance

Who built this and why it matters.

The UMI Initiative and the VELANA Project are not the same entity. Understanding the separation is important before engaging with either.

UMI Initiative
Non-commercial stewardship construct
The UMI Initiative holds stewardship of the open standard. It is not a legal entity. It does not charge for the standard, hold equity, or operate commercially. Current stewardship is provided by Aliventi sp. z o.o. until such time as adoption justifies a more formal governance structure. The standard is Apache 2.0. It cannot be made proprietary.
VELANA Project / Aliventi sp. z o.o.
Commercial implementation company
Aliventi sp. z o.o. is the Polish registered company that develops MILANA, AiQUS, and the VELANA governed record. It provides stewardship of the UMI Initiative as a separate, non-commercial function. Commercial enquiries, partnership discussions, and pilot access go through Aliventi. The UMI standard itself does not.
